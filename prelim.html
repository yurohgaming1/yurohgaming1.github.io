    What is BS CpE?

    A Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering is an undergraduate academic degree program that focuses on the intersection of computer science and electrical engineering. It is designed to provide students with a comprehensive education in both hardware and software aspects of computing systems.

    Hardware and Software Integration: The primary objective of a BS CpE program is to equip students with a comprehensive understanding of both hardware and software aspects of computer systems. This includes computer architecture, digital logic design, programming languages, and operating systems.

    Problem-Solving Skills: Students learn to analyze complex problems and develop innovative solutions that involve the design, implementation, and optimization of computer systems. These problem-solving skills are essential for addressing real-world challenges in technology.
    Hands-On Experience: Practical experience is a crucial component of the program. Students often engage in hands-on projects, labs, and internships to apply their theoretical knowledge to real-world scenarios, fostering creativity and technical proficiency.
    Ethical and Professional Development: BS CpE programs typically emphasize ethical conduct and professional responsibility in the field of computer engineering. Graduates are expected to adhere to ethical standards and contribute positively to society.

    Digital Logic Design: Understanding the fundamental building blocks of digital circuits, which are essential for computer hardware design.
    Microprocessors and Computer Architecture: Exploring the inner workings of CPUs and computer systems, including memory management and instruction pipelines.
    Programming and Software Engineering: Learning programming languages and software development methodologies to create applications and systems.
    Embedded Systems: Designing and programming small-scale computer systems for specific applications like IoT devices and robotics.
    Networking: Studying the principles of computer networking and communication protocols.
    Operating Systems: Understanding the core concepts of OS design and operation.
    Signal Processing: Learning how to process and manipulate digital signals, often used in multimedia and communication systems.

    Technology Advancements: In an increasingly digital world, computer engineers are at the forefront of technological advancements. They drive innovation by designing and improving computer systems and applications.
    Diverse Career Opportunities: Graduates of BS CpE programs have a wide array of career opportunities in industries such as IT, telecommunications, electronics, automotive, aerospace, and more. They can work as hardware engineers, software developers, network engineers, system architects, and more.
    Problem Solvers: Computer engineers are problem solvers by nature. They tackle complex challenges, from improving the efficiency of microprocessors to creating secure and robust software applications.
    Technological Impact: Computer engineers play a crucial role in shaping the future, contributing to developments in artificial intelligence, automation, cybersecurity, and sustainable technologies.

    Specializations

Embedded Systems

Specializing in embedded systems involves designing and programming computer systems integrated into various devices, such as microcontrollers and IoT gadgets.

Cybersecurity

This specialization focuses on safeguarding computer systems and networks from cyber threats. Topics include ethical hacking, digital forensics, cryptography, and security policy development.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Students delve into AI and machine learning, gaining expertise in algorithms, neural networks, and applications in fields like natural language processing and computer vision.

Network Engineering

Network engineering concentrates on the design, implementation, and management of computer networks. Students learn about network protocols, wireless communication and security.

Software Development

Specializing in software development involves creating applications and systems for various platforms. Topics include programming languages, software design, testing, and maintenance.

Robotics and Automation

This specialization delves into the design, programming, and automation of robotic systems, applicable in manufacturing, healthcare, autonomous vehicles, and more.

VLSI Design

VLSI design focuses on developing integrated circuits and microchips. Students cover chip design methodologies, transistor-level design, and semiconductor manufacturing processes.

Game Development

This specialization combines computer engineering and game design, focusing on graphics programming, game engine development and immersive gameplay experiences.

IoT (Internet of Things)

Specializing in IoT, students work on creating and connecting smart, interconnected devices and systems. Topics include sensor networks, IoT protocols, and data analytics for IoT applications.

    About the Department

MISSION

The Department of Computer Engineering declares its role to produce competitive engineers who are knowledgeable in the fundamental areas of digital and computer system design, software development, and modeling of physical systems.

VISION

The Department of Computer Engineering will impact the information age as a national leader in computing, research, and education.

PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

    After three to five years from graduation, the Computer Engineering alumni shall:

    1. Have pursued advancement towards becoming globally competitive leaders in their chosen field of practice

2. Be a contributor to the development of a progressive society, guided by the UE core values of Excellence, Integrity, Professionalism, Teamwork, Commitment, Transparency, Accountability and Social Responsibility.
